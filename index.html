Anonymized Case Study 01 – Institution-Scale Public Platform

A public-sector transformation program needed a workflow-first digital platform to support diversification journeys for a very large beneficiary base.

View details
Business context

A public-sector transformation program needed a workflow-first digital platform to support diversification journeys for a very large beneficiary base. The target operating model covered an initial rollout to 10,000+ institutions with a long-term nationwide scale vision, while also supporting activity discovery, DPR generation, financing progress, training access, and support requests. The platform design followed a 30-institution pilot across 3 states, where 29 DPRs worth ₹21.27 crore had been sanctioned and ₹1.02 crore disbursed.

Problem complexity

The problem was not just digitization. It involved turning fragmented offline journeys into a structured national workflow spanning profiling, activity selection, approvals, DPR preparation, loan journey tracking, milestone-based disbursement, evidence capture, dashboards, and future integrations.

Stakeholders involved

Program administrators, field partners, finance institutions, reviewers, platform users, and monitoring stakeholders across regional and central levels.

My exact responsibilities

Owned business requirement structuring, workflow decomposition, role mapping, module definition, validation logic, task flow design, document handling logic, dashboard expectations, and integration-readiness considerations for a multi-role platform.

Challenges faced

Key challenges included building for large-scale adoption, handling role-based access and maker-checker controls, accommodating structured manual operations before external integrations matured, and ensuring the platform remained scalable rather than over-engineered in the first release.

Solution approach

Defined a modular workflow backbone covering role-based login, profile management, activity repository, guided DPR generation, submission workflow, financing status tracking, support request handling, learning hub, dashboards, notifications, document versioning, audit trail expectations, and phased integrations.

Impact

Created a scalable business blueprint for a platform intended for 10,000+ institutions in phase one, with 12 core modules and a 16-step end-to-end operating workflow. The broader program context included a 30-institution pilot across 3 states, with 29 DPRs worth ₹21.27 crore sanctioned and ₹1.02 crore disbursed.

Anonymized Case Study 05 – Distressed Asset Settlement & Recovery Workflow

A large lending institution wanted to move compromise settlement handling away from manual and semi-automated operations into a workflow-driven module integrated with its broader legal and recovery platform.

View details
Business context

A large lending institution wanted to move compromise settlement handling away from manual and semi-automated operations into a workflow-driven module integrated with its broader legal and recovery platform. The goal was to manage proposals for NPA accounts with better visibility across hierarchy levels and faster turnaround on sanctions, reversions, tracking, and payment follow-up.

Problem complexity

The process sat at the intersection of recovery, legal status, and approval governance. Settlement proposals depended on upstream case context such as SARFAESI action, DRT or suit-filed status, and insolvency status, while downstream users needed dashboards, branch-to-head-office drilldowns, sanction tracking, recommendation logic, reports, and live status visibility.

Stakeholders involved

Branch users, regional reviewers, zonal reviewers, central approvers, legal and recovery teams, and operations stakeholders monitoring settlement pipelines.

My exact responsibilities

Defined end-to-end requirements for the settlement workflow, including dashboard expectations across four hierarchy levels, proposal form structure, recovery-action capture, recommendation and sanction stages, search and drilldown behavior, reports, notification expectations, and payment follow-up logic.

Challenges faced

The key challenge was converting a subjective and document-heavy approval process into a structured digital journey without losing flexibility for hierarchy-specific decisioning. The workflow had to reduce physical movement, reuse existing platform data, and provide consistent visibility from branch to central office.

Solution approach

Structured a settlement module integrated with the legal management platform, with 7 proposal tabs, hierarchy-based dashboards, auto-populated data from the parent platform, search and drilldown capabilities, tracking states, reporting, and follow-up workflows tied to proposal and payment status.

Impact

Defined a digitized recovery workflow that reduced reliance on manual handoffs, improved live-status visibility across 4 organizational levels, and created a more controlled process for settlement review, sanction, rejection, reversion, and payment tracking.

A financial-services legal and recovery function needed dedicated modules to monitor insolvency proceedings, litigation against the institution, and secured-recovery actions through a single structured operating model.

Business context

A financial-services legal and recovery function needed dedicated modules to monitor insolvency proceedings, litigation against the institution, and secured-recovery actions through a single structured operating model rather than fragmented case tracking.

Problem complexity

The problem included multiple legal journeys at once: insolvency proceedings, liquidation, complainant/respondent management, litigation formats, DRT stages, SARFAESI permissions and actions, hearing updates, court orders, and post-judgment follow-up. It also required role-sensitive views, maker-checker controls, ETL-fed account visibility, remarks management, and dashboards for branch, regional, zonal, and central teams.

Stakeholders involved

Legal teams, recovery teams, branch makers and checkers, regional and zonal reviewers, central monitoring teams, advocates, and external legal process participants.

My exact responsibilities

Owned requirement interpretation and workflow design for legal-monitoring modules covering case intake, field definitions, validation rules, role-based screens, ETL-driven account display, litigation detail capture, insolvency and liquidation status tracking, hearing/order management, dashboard metrics, and report expectations.

Challenges faced

The biggest challenge was designing around legal-process variability while preserving structured monitoring. The system had to handle different case types, stage-dependent field behavior, extensive validation, role-specific edit rights, and drilldown reporting without making the user journey too fragmented.

Solution approach

Translated legal and insolvency processes into dedicated modules for insolvency monitoring, complainant/respondent tracking, litigation case formats, DRT and SARFAESI process capture, recovery and liquidation tracking, remarks and hearing management, and dashboard visibility across organizational levels.

Impact

Created the blueprint for a more unified legal-monitoring ecosystem with maker-checker governance, ETL-supported account visibility, customer-to-head-office drilldowns, and structured dashboards covering insolvency, litigation, recovery progress, liquidation values, and case-stage monitoring.
